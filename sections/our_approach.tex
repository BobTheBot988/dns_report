\section{Overview of your proposed approach}\label{sec:our_approach}
\subsection{Technical Architecture}
We propose \textbf{Meshtastic}, a decentralized mesh network utilizing the \textbf{LoRa physical layer} and Chirp Spread Spectrum (CSS) [5]. Signals consist of frequency-sweeping "chirps" with a 16-up-chirp Preamble for synchronization and a specific Sync Word (0x2B) to distinguish the network [5, 10].

\subsection{Decentralized Metadata Protection}
The primary strength is the total bypass of Cellular Operator Data Systems [5]. In a mesh architecture, there is no central database logging "Top 2 BS" anchors or hourly traffic windows—the features required by SURE and MoCo for re-identification [3, 5].

\subsection{Encryption and Routing}
Meshtastic payloads are encrypted using \textbf{AES-128 shared-key encryption} [5, 11]. Only nodes with the correct channel key can interpret data, while decentralized "hop limits" prevent the creation of a trackable historical footprint [5, 12].

\section{Evaluation}\label{sec:evaluation}

\subsection{Proposed Experimental Setup}
We propose comparing a control group on a cellular network against a test group using Meshtastic over 7 days \cite{duan2025sure, meshtastic_overview}. We will apply the \textbf{SURE architecture} to match anonymous traces back to a leaked identity database \cite{duan2025sure}. The experimental design follows the methodology established in the original SURE evaluation: the control group's cellular traces will be collected via operator BSS/OSS in the standard format (user ID, BS ID, timestamps, uplink/downlink traffic), while the test group will communicate exclusively through the Meshtastic mesh network. Both groups will perform equivalent daily routines to ensure behavioral comparability. The SURE model, pre-trained on known cellular trace data, will then attempt to re-identify users in both groups.

\subsection{Performance Metrics}
Success will be measured via \textbf{AUC}, F1-Score, and Precision-Recall \cite{duan2025sure, duan2025moco}. A successful mitigation will show the Meshtastic group's re-identification AUC dropping to near-random levels ($\approx 0.5$) compared to the control group's $\ge 0.9$ \cite{duan2025sure}. Additionally, we will apply MoCo's contact detection pipeline \cite{duan2025moco} to evaluate whether co-traveling users within the Meshtastic group can still be identified through their movement patterns. The expected outcome is that, without access to cellular signaling traces, MoCo's F1-score should degrade significantly from the reported 87.13\% baseline.

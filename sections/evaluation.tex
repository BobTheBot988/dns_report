\section{Evaluation}\label{sec:evaluation}

\subsection{Proposed Experimental Setup}
We propose comparing a control group on a cellular network against a test group using Meshtastic over 7 days [3, 6]. We will apply the \textbf{SURE architecture} to match anonymous traces back to a leaked identity database [2, 3].

\subsection{Performance Metrics}
Success will be measured via \textbf{AUC}, F1-Score, and Precision-Recall [2, 4]. A successful mitigation will show the Meshtastic group's re-identification AUC dropping to near-random levels compared to the control group’s $\ge$ 0.9 [2, 3].

\section{Related Work}
\subsection{The Collapse of K-Anonymity}
Removing names is useless if spatial behavior—the "Top 2 BS" routine—remains stable [1, 3]. Mathematical proof shows male targets and those over 45 are the easiest to track due to stable logistical routines [1, 3].

\subsection{Alternative SSL Defenses}
Techniques like \textbf{ASTRA} (Adaptive-Attacks) and \textbf{RoCL} (Robust Contrastive Learning) harden SSL models against perturbations [14, 15]. However, these do not address the root cause of tracking: centralized metadata logging [3, 6].
